## §1. A re-score per axis

### R1 Motivation — **3** (v1: 3; Δ = 0)

**v2 rubric text.**  Cycle #10 band-3 text + M10.2 tightening:
named-precedent citation caps at band 2 without (a) shape match
or (b) motivation-section obstruction sketch.

**Evidence from A §1 (unchanged content).**

- §1(a) **Duplicated-inverses argument:** "`exp` / `ln` are the
  only genuinely new data; the other 'inverses' are expressible
  once a single complex unit is available." — this **argues
  irreducibility of exp and ln** as the "genuinely new" data
  that cannot be reduced to inverse-of-existing primitives.
- §1(b) **Real-vs-complex fusion:** "Admitting `i` collapses a
  whole tower of primitives onto one." — structural identity.
- §1(c) **Field-generation precedent:** "A field is generated by
  two operations, `+` and `×`, plus one constant that is not a
  root of its prime subfield.  Arithmetic of ℂ has a similar
  minimal backbone; **everything transcendental lies on top**."

**Tightening application.**

- **Trigger.** A cites field-generation as a minimal-generator
  precedent (2 primitives + 1 constant shape).  The tightening's
  trigger ("named structural precedent for single-primitive or
  single-generator reduction ... or a functionally equivalent
  ... minimal-generator analog") holds.
- **(a) Shape match check.** A's strict minimum (§4): 2 binary +
  2 unary + 1 constant = 5.  A's relaxed minimum (§3.8):
  1 binary + 2 unary + 1 constant = 4.  Field-precedent shape:
  2 binary + 1 constant = 3.  Strict A has 2 additional unary
  helpers; relaxed A has 1 additional binary reduction + 2 unary
  helpers.  **(a) fails.**
- **(b) Obstruction sketch check.** A's §1(a) argues that exp
  and ln are "the only genuinely new data" — irreducible data
  that cannot be reduced to inverses of existing primitives.
  Combined with §1(c) "everything transcendental lies on top" —
  the sketch is: field backbone covers arithmetic; transcendentals
  (exp/ln) are irreducible additions that must sit on top.  This
  is a **sketch-level obstruction argument** for why the final
  basis must exceed the field-precedent shape.  Not a full proof
  — that lives in §3.6 — but the motivation section itself
  contains the sketch.  **(b) met at sketch level.**
- **Cap assessment:** (b) met → no cap applies.  R1 stays at
  band 3.

**Distinction from X (important rubric discipline).**  X's §1
closed with: "the reduction pressure is therefore on the
transcendental side, with the arithmetic side fixed to a single
binary operation."  X's closure is a *strategy* statement ("we'll
focus here"), not an *obstruction* argument ("exp/ln are
irreducible because Y").  A's §1(a) explicitly argues exp/ln as
"the only genuinely new data" (irreducibility claim); X's §1
does not argue irreducibility anywhere in the motivation section.
Under the v2 tightening, this distinction is load-bearing: A
clears the obstruction-sketch bar; X does not.

**Verdict: R1 v2 = 3.**  Δ from v1: **0**.

## §4. Retrospective finding

The cycle-10 A and B deliverables are **unaffected** by the
Cycle #11 R1/R2 tightening.  This confirms the tightening's
**non-inflation design**: deliverables that supply motivation-
answer consistency (A §1(a) + §1(c) sketches obstruction) and
per-sublemma proof locality (both A §3.6 and B §4 have per-sublemma
proof blocks) retain their v1 bands.

**Contrast with X retrospective** (`cycle-11/X-JUDGMENT-v2.md`):
X drops R1 3→2 and R2 3→2 because X fails both obstruction sketch
(R1 (b)) and proof locality (R2 (b)).  The tightening
discriminates against the specific fault patterns the Cycle #10
falsification test surfaced (motivation-answer mismatch; sublemma
declared without per-sublemma proof), while leaving correctly-
structured deliverables unchanged.

Empirical implication for the cycle-log: the R1/R2 tightening is
**backward-compatible** with cycle-10 A/B, matching the
backward-compatibility posture of M8.1/M8.2 locus ports (cycle-09
retrospective) and M8.3/M8.4/M6.3 (a) ports (cycle-08 retrospective).
