Example 5: critical-pair / overlap audit
========================================================================
Each rule's LHS pattern is headed by a distinct primitive symbol.
LHS shapes (as spine head, arity):
  I: head=I, arity=1
  K: head=K, arity=2
  S: head=S, arity=3
  B: head=B, arity=3
  C: head=C, arity=3
  W: head=W, arity=2
  M: head=M, arity=1
  Y: head=Y, arity=1
  T: head=T, arity=2
  V: head=V, arity=3
  D: head=D, arity=4
  P1: head=P1, arity=2
  P2: head=P2, arity=2

Two rules can produce a critical pair only when one rule's LHS
unifies with a non-variable subterm of another's LHS.  Each LHS
here is (PrimitiveSymbol  v1  ...  vk) where v_i are placeholder
metavariables.  The only non-variable subterms are the primitive
itself and the partial applications (Primitive v1), (Primitive v1 v2),
etc.  These all begin with the same primitive symbol; two distinct
primitives cannot unify.  Self-overlap reduces to root-overlap
(the trivial case).  Therefore: 0 non-trivial critical pairs.
